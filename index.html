About Me

Let me first introduce myself.
My name is Thea Saada, software engineer from Israel, mother of two beautiful children, Eden - age 24, and Tal - age 21.
My lifestyle includes strict discipline of early wakeup, strict nutrition and meeting competition goals.
I can say that I try to keep work-sport-life balance. I know how to translate the competitive nature of sports into life routine in general and career goals in particular. I like to travel the world and study different cultures. I like visiting restaurants and eat delicious food.
As athlete I started running. It soon became long distance running and I ended doing marathons and participated in public events and later on also races. That is how I have discovered I like racing and actually I am a very competitive person. After a few years running I started to discover the other side of sport, I have started cycling.
At first this was cheap MTB, riding with friends. Soon I moved to road bike and joined to new competitive, cycling team, IGP, which just emerged, led by senior experienced Israeli rider, Zachi Boygen. As of now, 15 years in IGP, personally trained by Zachi, train 4-6 times a week, participate in race.
A bonus of this sport - every year, around mid-July, the team travels to famous places in Europe for a week of cycling in the mountains, places like the Dolomites or Alps at Italy, France, Spain, Swiss. So, for me, team travel is an opportunity for a real vacation - time for myself, good friends and getting to know new friends, food and wine, beautiful famous places, and of course - cycling.
